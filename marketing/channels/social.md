# Social Media Playbook

Three platforms. One voice. Low cadence.

We will not be on every platform. We will not post daily. We will post when we have something *worth* posting — usually a 4–8 second clip that makes you want to play it.

## Platform priorities

| Platform | Priority | Cadence | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Twitter/X | High | 2–4×/week | #screenshotsaturday, #pygame, #IndieDev community |
| Mastodon (mastodon.gamedev.place) | High | 2–3×/week | Aligned indie audience, no algo suppression |
| Bluesky | Medium | 2×/week | Growing dev community, low friction crosspost |
| YouTube | Medium-High | 1–2×/week | Painted look reads in motion; soundtrack lives here |
| TikTok | **Skip** | — | Wrong vibe; we're warm, not viral |
| Instagram | **Skip** | — | Static images undersell the motion |
| LinkedIn | **Skip** | — | We are not a B2B SaaS |
| Discord | See `community.md` | — | Owned community, separate playbook |

## Posting cadence

### Weekly rhythm
- **Saturday** — #screenshotsaturday clip (the anchor post)
- **Tuesday** — devlog excerpt or "what I'm working on"
- **Thursday** — flexible: music sample, level-design note, fan post, or skip

### Monthly rhythm
- **Last Friday of month** — public-facing metrics post (transparent numbers + lessons)

## Post templates

### Template A: The clip drop (Saturdays)
```
[6-second GIF or MP4]

[One short sentence in the voice — see brand/voice.md]

[link to itch.io or repo]

#screenshotsaturday #IndieDev #pygame
```

Examples:
- "Tangle Jungle, 1-2. Found a path I didn't expect."
- "Roll attack lands different when you string two together."
- "The waterfall hides something. Let it."

### Template B: The devlog snippet (Tuesdays)
```
Working on [thing]. The hard part was [specific challenge].

[Optional: code snippet, GIF, or before/after]

Full devlog: [link]
```

### Template C: The music share (Thursdays — when there's music)
```
30 seconds of the [Tangle Jungle / Boss] theme. Headphones recommended.

[YouTube link to the music-only video, or SoundCloud embed]

#GameMusic #IndieDev
```

### Template D: The community boost
```
[Quote or screenshot of a player's run / route / fork / fan art]

[One line of genuine appreciation, not corporate "thank you for being part of our journey"]
```

### Template E: The art reveal (rare, planned)
```
Meet [character name].

[Painted concept art]

[2-3 sentences about who they are in the world]
```

## Don't post

- Birthday/holiday content (we are not a brand)
- "Big announcement coming!" teasers (just announce the thing)
- Polls for engagement
- "Drop a 🔥 if you agree" cringe
- Engagement-bait threads
- Anything that mentions a Nintendo property by name in a comparison

## Hashtag strategy

| Hashtag | Use on | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| `#screenshotsaturday` | Saturdays only | Anchor post |
| `#IndieDev` | All gameplay clips | Most posts |
| `#pygame` | Tech-flavored posts | Sometimes |
| `#madewithpygame` | Devlog posts | Sometimes |
| `#gamedev` | Strong gameplay clips | Sometimes |
| `#GameMusic` | Music-share posts | Music posts only |
| `#screenshotsunday` | Don't | — |
| `#DonkeyKong` / `#DKC` | Don't, ever | — |

Max 3 hashtags per post. More than 3 reads as desperate.

## Visual requirements

Every post that's not text-only must have:
- Real in-engine motion or screenshot (no mockups)
- Tagline or single sentence (no walls of copy)
- Loop length 4–8 seconds for GIF/MP4
- HUD visible (the HUD is part of the brand — see `brand/visuals.md`)

## YouTube specifically

Two video types, two purposes:

| Type | Length | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Gameplay trailer | 60–90s | Drive itch.io / Steam page visits |
| Music-only video | 2–4 min | Reach the game-music audience (Persona C in `audience.md`) |
| Devlog short | 30–60s | Build personal connection, recurring weekly slot |

Long-form (10+ min) devlogs are out of scope until we have material worth that length.

## Reply / engagement etiquette

- **Always reply within 24h** in the first month post-launch. Drops to 48h after.
- **Reply with personality** — see brand voice. Don't sound like a community manager.
- **Like, don't quote-retweet** kind comments. Quote-retweet only for funny/notable additions.
- **Never argue.** If someone says "this is just *DKC*," reply once with a friendly clarification ("DKC was a big inspiration; everything in *Ape Adventure* is original IP — different characters, different worlds, different studio"), then disengage.

## Crosspost flow

```
Primary: write on Mastodon (no character limit pressure)
  ↓
Adapt: shorten for Twitter/Bluesky (use the same media asset)
  ↓
Repackage: stretch into a YouTube short if the clip is strong
  ↓
Pin: anchor weekly thread on Mastodon for newcomers
```

## Metrics that matter

| Metric | Why we watch | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Click-through to itch/GitHub | Conversion to play/star | >2% of impressions |
| Replies (vs likes) | Quality of engagement | Replies > likes/10 = good |
| Saves / bookmarks | Lasting interest | Any volume = good signal |
| YouTube music-video listen-through | Reach to Persona C | >60% of viewers reach 1:00 mark |
| Follower growth | Audience building | >25/week organic = good |

Followers are a vanity metric on their own. Click-through, saves, and listen-through are the real signals.

## Quarterly review

Every quarter, ask:
1. Which 3 posts worked best? Why?
2. Which platform delivered most click-throughs?
3. Should we drop a platform that isn't earning its time?
4. Is the voice still consistent with `brand/voice.md`?
5. Are we still avoiding IP-trap language?
