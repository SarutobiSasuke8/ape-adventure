## One-liner

A 90s-style jungle platformer where one ape takes on a Saurian invasion.

## Short description (50 words)

*Ape Adventure* is a 90s-console-style jungle platformer with painted backdrops, a real soundtrack, and momentum-led platforming. Run, roll, swing, and rescue your way through Tangle Jungle while the Saurians try to stop you. Original IP, hand-crafted, single-player. Vertical slice playable now.

## Long description (150 words)

*Ape Adventure* is a love letter to 1990s console platformers — built fresh, with original characters, original IP, and a painted look that takes its cues from the SNES era without imitating any one game.

You play Bongo, a young ape whose home — Tangle Jungle — has been invaded by the Saurians, a faction of lizard-folk led by King Saurus. Run, roll, swing through vines, smash crates, collect bananas, and find the AAPE letters hidden in every level. Each world ends with a boss fight that's earned, not forced.

The vertical slice (World 1: Tangle Jungle) is playable now: 4 levels, 1 boss, ~30 minutes of game. We're sharing this build to find out if the feel works before committing to the full 6-world adventure.

The music is the brand. Trust your ears.

## Features (vertical slice)

- **One painted world.** Tangle Jungle: canopy, vines, hollow logs, a waterfall
- **Four levels** including a 3-phase boss fight
- **Tight 90s-feel platforming.** Coyote time, jump buffer, variable-height jumps
- **Roll attack.** A momentum-preserving dash that defeats most ground enemies
- **Collectibles.** Bananas, AAPE letters (one per level), bonus barrels, banana bunches
- **Real soundtrack.** Looping atmospheric jungle theme, distinct boss theme
- **Hidden secrets.** Bonus barrels reveal optional skill challenges
- **Local saves.** Progress persisted on your machine, no accounts

## History (3 sentences)

The project began as a single-file arcade homage (preserved as the legacy *Ape Escape*) before pivoting in early 2026 to a 90s-console-platformer direction. The vertical slice took 8 weeks of focused development and is shared in the open as a "does this feel work?" check before Stage 2. Our hope is to build the full 6-world adventure if the community engages with the slice.
