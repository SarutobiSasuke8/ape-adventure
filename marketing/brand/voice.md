# Voice & Tone

## Voice (constant)

**Adventurous. Warm. Vivid.**

We sound like a friend who just got back from somewhere amazing and wants to tell you about it — without bragging, without overselling. Sensory language. Active verbs. Concrete details over adjectives.

This is a deliberate **shift** from the legacy *Ape Escape* voice, which was dry and arcade-terse. *Ape Adventure* is meant to be inviting, not aloof.

## Tone (situational)

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Trailer / hero copy | Cinematic, evocative | "The canopy hums. The river runs gold. Then the Saurians arrive." |
| Store description | Warm, factual | "A 90s-style jungle platformer with painted backdrops and a soundtrack that earns your attention." |
| Social posts | Curious, playful | "Found a bonus barrel behind the waterfall on 1-2. Took me three tries." |
| Devlogs | Honest, craft-focused | "I wanted Bongo's run cycle to feel weighted on the down-beat. Here's how the timing landed." |
| Patch notes | Friendly, specific | "Slingers no longer launch coconuts through walls. The jungle is safer, mostly." |
| Replies / community | Genuine, curious | "Nice route on 1-3 — did you find the second bonus barrel?" |

## Words we use

run · roll · swing · pounce · canopy · jungle · vine · waterfall · totem · soundtrack · momentum · rhythm · secret · hidden · painted · 90s · SNES-era · platformer · adventure · Bongo · Saurian · banana · AAPE · bonus barrel

## Words we don't use

epic · immersive · revolutionary · disrupting · reimagined · journey · experience · AAA · Web3 · NFT · play-to-earn · grind · roguelite · metroidvania (we're neither) · pixel-perfect (we're not pixel-art) · retro (we're 90s, which is its own thing) · soulslike

## Words to **especially** avoid (IP risk)

Donkey · Kong · Diddy · Kremling · K. Rool · Banana Coin · KONG letter · Cranky · Funky · Wrinkly · Candy · Animal Buddy · Rambi · Enguarde

We can say "DKC-inspired" in a devlog, once, in context. We never say it in marketing copy.

## House style

- **Sentence case** in headlines. ("Run. Roll. Rescue." is a tagline; not a sentence.)
- **One exclamation point per page**, max. Save it for the title or a moment of real surprise.
- **Specific over generic.** "The water shimmers in 1-2" beats "beautiful environments."
- **Show, don't tell.** A 6-second clip beats any sentence.
- **Numbers ground claims.** "12 enemies, 3 phases, 1 boss in the slice" is more interesting than "rich gameplay."

## Voice test

Before publishing copy, ask:
1. Could the publisher of any indie platformer have written this? → Rewrite.
2. Does it sound like a press release? → Rewrite.
3. Would the 90s-kid-grown-up (see `brand/identity.md`) actually say it? → Ship it.
4. Does it accidentally invoke a Nintendo trademark? → Rewrite immediately.
