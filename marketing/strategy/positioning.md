# Positioning

## Positioning statement

> For **platformer fans who grew up on 90s consoles** and **indie game enthusiasts who value craft over scope**,
> **Ape Adventure** is a **modern jungle platformer in the SNES-era tradition** that delivers **painted visuals, a soundtrack that earns repeat play, and rewarding secrets** —
> unlike **pixel-art retro throwbacks or photorealistic 3D platformers**, our game lives in the **painted, momentum-first 90s console aesthetic** — original IP, hand-crafted, no clones.

## Value proposition (the elevator pitch)

> **A jungle. An ape. A Saurian invasion.** *Ape Adventure* is a 90s-style platformer with original characters, painted backdrops, and a soundtrack you'll hum for weeks. **Run. Roll. Rescue.**

## Three things to say in every conversation

1. **It's the 90s console feel without the costume.** Painted, lush, atmospheric — not pixel-art-retro, not flat-vector, not photoreal.
2. **Original IP.** Bongo and the Saurians are ours. We're inspired by the genre, not riffing on anyone's trademarks.
3. **Soundtrack-led.** A 90s platformer is its music. We're treating the score as the lead instrument, not the garnish.

## Differentiators

| What people expect | What we actually do |
|---|---|
| "Is this pixel art?" | No — painted, layered, warm |
| "Is this a *DKC* fan game?" | No — original IP. DKC is one of many inspirations |
| "Is it photoreal 3D?" | No — 2D platformer in 90s console tradition |
| "Microtransactions / DLC?" | No |
| "Battle pass / season 2?" | No |
| "Online leaderboards?" | Local saves only |
| "Story / cutscenes?" | Environmental only — no dialogue, no cutscenes |

## Positioning vs. the legacy v0 (Ape Escape)

The legacy game positioned on **constraint** ("single file, no assets, vector clean"). The new project positions on **craft and warmth** ("painted, soundtracked, hand-built worlds"). They're emotional opposites and that's intentional — *Ape Escape* was tense and arcade; *Ape Adventure* is warm and exploratory.

## Anti-positioning (claims we deliberately won't make)

- "AAA-quality on a budget" — we're not chasing AAA.
- "Hardcore difficulty" — we're not soulslike. We're momentum-first.
- "The next *DKC*" — we will *never* compare ourselves to a Nintendo property.
- "Innovative new genre" — we're refining a beloved one. The innovation is in the polish, not the formula.
- "Pixel-perfect retro" — we're not pixel art.
- "Procedural / endless" — we're hand-crafted levels.

## Brand-as-positioning

Our positioning lives in *contrast pairs*:
- Painted, **not** pixel
- Original, **not** clone
- Momentum, **not** combat
- Hand-crafted, **not** procedural
- Soundtracked, **not** ambient
- 90s feel, **not** retro costume

## What we are *competing* with for player attention

We don't compete with AAA. We compete with the player's:
- Saturday afternoon between errands
- Their kid wanting to "play something fun" together
- The Steam library purchase they keep meaning to start
- The nostalgia for a game they used to own

This shapes everything: short levels, generous checkpoints, clear language, a soundtrack that pulls them back.

## Vertical slice positioning (specific)

For the **vertical slice launch** (1 world, 4 levels, 1 boss):

> "A first taste of *Ape Adventure* — Tangle Jungle, the opening world of a 90s-style platformer in the works. Free, playable, ~30 minutes. We're sharing this build to find out if the feel works before we build the rest."

Position the slice as **honest in-progress sharing**, not a demo gate. This audience appreciates being told the truth.
